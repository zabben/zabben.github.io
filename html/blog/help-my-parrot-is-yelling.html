Help! My parrot is yelling

If you have parrot, it's going to be loud. They usually yell twice a day; once in the morning and once in the evening. There are times though when a parrot yells more than that and this can have a wide variety of reasons. It can be related to their cage, food or health. The first thing to remember with parrots is this: keep it occupied and do not engage when it yells. An occupied parrot is less likely to yell than a bored one. Besides this, these are a couple of guidelines to keep in mind.

Cage

The parrots cage should be entertaining for them. Stuff it with branches for them to gnaw and climb on and with toys to play with. Don't keep the same toys in the cage for a long time: rotate. Some parrots are bored easily and need something new every once in a while. Don't overstuff though; like us humans, parrots like their breathing space as well.

Food

Concerning food, you can try to keep your parrot occupied by hiding food in specific toys or by changing its diet. They might just be hungry or don't like the food you are usually giving them, sometimes the solution is easier than the problem. The pet shop has many toys for parrots that you can stuff food in so the parrot can only get to it after some effort. This keeps them occupied and they get a reward.

Health

The parrot might yell because it is not feeling well. Check you parrot for unusual behavorial patterns or weight loss and don't hesitate to visit a doctor.

Training

Toys are not enough. You have to actively play with and train your bird. Learn them tricks, like stepping up (pretty basic) and flying around the room to land on a specific spot. Some parrots even like to shower with you. Spice up its live, keep it occupied!
