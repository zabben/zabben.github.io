Eclectus

What probably stands out most about the eclectus parrot is its sexual dimorphism of the colours of the feathers, which doesn't happen very often with pet birds. Male eclectus parrots are emerald green, female eclectus parrots are bright red. This confused zoologists so much that at first they even thought they were entirely different birds.
They make for fun and calm pets, but they tend to get scared of new situations, which can cause problem behaviour such as picking and cutting. If you're bringing your eclectus in a novel situation, make sure to comfort it or to make the changes one by one, step by step.

The eclectus as a pet bird is relatively new, is hasn't been in captivity since the 1980s. It is therefore not yet entirely possible to measure its average lifespan, but it's safe to assume an average between 25 and 40 years.
