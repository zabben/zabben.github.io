Lovebird

Is there any bird more romantic than the lovebird? It's also known as agapornis but we prefer to stick to the lovebird. These social little creatures get this name because of their strong, monogamous pair bonding and the long period of time they spend sitting together. Don't mistake this monogamous behaviour with inertness though, their quite the little energetic adventurers. Lovebirds don't only bond with other lovebirds though, they can also get especially attached to other birds and humans. This attachment might lead to mean behaviour like biting, so beware! Do make sure your lovebird has a buddy if you don't have the time to give it all the specific attention it needs.

Lovebirds are noisy little creatures, with sounds ranging from gentle tweets to highly irritating and loud. They need these loud sounds in the wild, where they use them to keep their flock together.
