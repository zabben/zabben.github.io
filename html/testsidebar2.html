While most people think of the macaw when when they hear the word parrot, the African grey parrot is actually the most commonly kept large parrot in homes around the world. Outside of our human homes, millions of African greys can be found in their natural habitat in equatorial African, but their numbers have been declining for many years.
African greys make great pets. They require a lot of your time and attention, but the rewards are aplenty. While sassy and sometimes moody, greys are fun and have strong personalities.

There are two kinds of African greys: Congo parrots (slightly bigger) and Timneh parrots. The Congo is roughly 15 inches long and weighs 500 grams, the Timneh 11 inches and weighs 300 grams. Like most large parrots, they are bound to be your companion for life: their lifespan ranges from 40 to 70 years.
